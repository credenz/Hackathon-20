IDEATHON'20

About

The modern world is now more connected and online than ever before. The latest technological developments are revolutionizing the planet by increasing the ease and accessibility of services for every person.

At Ideathon 2020, we are inviting all developers and hackathon enthusiasts to come up with fantastic ideas for changing the world. The exciting prizes will be handed out to the prizes with the most innovative ideas and fantastic pitches. This Ideathon is open to all developers who have an eye for design and passion to innovate. Participants can either hack as a team of upto 5 participants. Take advantage of this opportunity to learn with other teams, showcase your talent, and compete for prizes!

Rules

• Team Size: 1 – 5 members
• Domain: Must be one of the 8 domains provided by us.
• Rounds:
There will be 2 Rounds:

• Round 1: PPT Submission – The participant teams will email their ppts at ___@pict.edu by Monday 9th November 2020 and the top 10 teams will be selected from them on the basis of idea, presentation and innovation.

• Round2: Pitching – Each team will get 10 minutes to present their idea on Friday, 13th November 2020 in front of a panel of judges and the winners will be selected on the basis of innovation, clarity of pitch and potential for the idea.

Submission Guidelines:
• Only 1 PowerPoint presentation must be made by each team and is to be used for both rounds. The first slide must contain the team name, product name, participant names, college/company and domain of product. The presentation must not exceed 10 slides, and will be judged on the basis of:
• Problem and Statement
• Solution
• Architecture Diagram
• Business Model
• Market
• Execution Plan
• Tech Stack
• Future Scope

FAQ's

Q1. Does the PPT need to follow a particular format?

No, as far as the requirements are covered in the PPT, you can use any format and colours in the PPT. Please refer Submission Guidelines.

Q2. How many members can take part in a team?

Minimum 1 person and maximum 5 persons per team.

Q3. Do we need to pay any registration fee for the Ideathon?

No, there is no registration fee.

Q4. Can we use a video instead of pitching the idea live?

Unless you are facing serious technical issues, we will require live presentation in the final round.

Q5. Can the participants be working professionals or non-students?

Yes, we welcome participants from every area and qualification. You can also make teams consisting of a mixture of students and professionals, from different colleges and sectors.

Open Innovation

This track welcomes ideas from participants solving problems in any domain of their choice. Enabling the community to broaden the scope of employment opportunities, remote surveillance, social benefits or any domain will be welcome. May the most innovative ideas win!

Health Care

The COVID-19 pandemic has starved the world of healthcare services, and has worsened the gap in accessibility tremendously. Many people around the world are limited in their access to necessary healthcare services, thereby increasing their risk of poor health outcomes and serious complications. This track is to encourage innovative healthcare solutions including diagnostic techniques, healthcare chat bots, products using connected technologies like wearables and smartphones, mental healthcare assistance or other solutions.

Education

The recent COVID-19 pandemic has brought a dramatic shift in the way we perceive education. Educational institutions have been challenged to transform their traditional teaching patterns to accommodate the remote scenario. In this track we encourage ideas that will revolutionize the field of remote learning, smart classrooms and interactive education.

Environment and Sustainability

The climate is the most important concern of this decade, as the consequences of our harmful activities are showing their effects on the Earth. From pollution detecting technologies to sustainable products, this track welcomes innovative solutions that will tackle climate change and create a greener world.

Automation

From smart offices to trendy kitchens, automation is revolutionizing the way we interact and get benefited from technological advancements. This track focuses on products that will integrate automation with personal and office spaces and use technologies to produce and deliver goods and services with minimal human intervention.

Banking and Fintech

In this track we are looking for ideas including by not limited to the adoption of mobile payment technologies, insurance fraud detection systems and financial management products for professionals. In short, we encourage products that will solve existing challenges in the banking sector, with the help of the latest technologies.

Cybersecurity

Cyber Security is one of growing areas in the industry, due to a rising amount of threats, malware and attacks over the internet. Interested participants will the opportunity to build robust cyber defense solutions in network security, database security, threat hunting, unified threat management systems and more.

Agriculture

India is a global agricultural powerhouse. From Kashmir to Tamil Nadu, farmers in India are some of the most neglected population segments with inadequate access to technology, financial resources and education. This track welcomes all ideas aiming to disrupt agriculture in the modern world, from remote crop monitoring solutions to effective yield estimation. We encourage solutions that could help farmers increase their profits, reduce their losses or that simply integrate technology with their daily activities while being cost-friendly and accessible.
